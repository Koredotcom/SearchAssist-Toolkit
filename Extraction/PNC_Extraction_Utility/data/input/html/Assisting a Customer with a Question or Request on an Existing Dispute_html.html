Assisting customers with questions or updates on existing disputes

If the customer has questions about an existing dispute, including the dispute outcome, or wants an update on an existing dispute, refer to the How To section below to assist the customer.\

 

\

Canceling a dispute

A customer may contact PNC to cancel a dispute they submitted. This usually happens when the customer realizes they authorized the transaction. You can only cancel a dispute if the customer requests it. Depending on the type of transaction, you’ll be able to process the cancellation or you’ll pass the request to another department to process. 

Explain to your customer that any credit or fee refunds issued to their account as a result of filing the dispute will be reversed within one to five business days of canceling the dispute.\

Cancel a debit/banking card, ACH or ATM dispute
\


Customers can request to cancel a dispute in person or over the phone in the branch or in the Customer Care Center.
 

\
  1. Identify the customer.
  2. Which system are you using to cancel the dispute?\
    • Genesis – From the customer's Contact or Business folder, select the deposit account that the dispute was submitted from. Click Disputes. 
    • SPIRIT - Access the Deposit Notebook in SPIRIT. Select the Trans History tab and click Disputes. 
    \
\
The Disputes Tool screen will display any disputable transactions and any prior adjustments and disputes.
 
\
  1. Locate the dispute in the Prior Disputes section.
  2. Click Cancel.
  3. Complete the Reason for Cancellation field with information provided by the customer, including why the customer is requesting the dispute be canceled.
  4. Select one of the following: \
    • Entire claim is to be canceled – Select if the customer wants to cancel the dispute for all transactions in the service request.
    • Only a portion of the claim is to be canceled - Select if the customer wants to cancel the dispute for one or more (but not all) of the transactions in the service request. You’ll be required to select the appropriate dates and amounts to be canceled.
    \
  5. Enter additional comments, if needed. 
  6. Click Submit. A Successful Response message will appear.
  7. Let your customer know they’ll receive a letter in the mail confirming the dispute has been canceled. Tell your customer that if PNC previously issued credit or fee refunds to their account related to the disputed transaction(s) being cancelled the credit or fee refunds will be reversed.
\

Cancel a check or teller withdrawal dispute
\


Customers can request to cancel a dispute in person or over the phone in the branch or in the Customer Care Center.
 

\
  1. Identify the customer.
  2. Which system are you using to cancel the dispute?\
    • Genesis - From the customer's Contact or Business folder, select the deposit account that the dispute was submitted from. Click Disputes. 
    • SPIRIT - Access the Deposit Notebook in SPIRIT. Select the Trans History tab and click Disputes.
    \
\
The Disputes Tool screen will display any disputable transactions and any prior adjustments and disputes.
 
\
  1. Locate the dispute in the Prior Disputes section. If the dispute hasn’t been assigned, you can add notes to the dispute to cancel it. Once it’s assigned, the partner will see the note to cancel and will cancel the dispute. You can tell your customer you’ve requested the dispute be canceled.
  2. Determine if the original dispute was due to fraud. You can ask the customer or look for words like "forged signature,” “unauthorized check,” “endorsement issue,” “check alterations,” “unauthorized signature” or “counterfeit” in the dispute title.\
    • If the transaction was disputed due to suspected fraud:\
      • For endorsement issues or check alterations, email the request to cancel the dispute to the Enterprise Fraud Group – Without Entry Claims Group. Let your customer know that their dispute will be canceled.
      • For all other suspected fraud issues, contact the Enterprise Fraud Group - Check Fraud Investigations. Let your customer know that their dispute will be canceled.
      \
    • If the original transaction wasn’t fraud related: Call the Bank Operations Support Center to cancel the dispute. Provide the service request number and the reason for cancellation to the Bank Operations Support Center representative. Let your customer know that their dispute will be canceled.
    \

Provide the customer with an update on an existing dispute

These steps apply to the following dispute types:
  • ACH credit
  • ACH debit (electronic withdrawal, other than PNC Online Banking)
  • Debit/banking card (ATM, PIN point-of-sale or non-PIN point-of-sale) or card-free (ATM) 
  • Deposit, check or teller withdrawal
  • Non-fraud check disputes
  • Digital cards in a mobile wallet (for example, Apple Pay)

If the dispute is open:\
  1. Advise the customer of the timeframe to resolve the dispute or issue provisional credit. Refer to the Timeframe for PNC to investigate and resolve customer debit/banking card disputes section of Disputing or Researching a Transaction Using the Disputes Tool.
  2. Review the notes in the dispute CRISS case and provide the customer with an update if available. If an update isn’t available, tell the customer the dispute is still in progress. Refer to the dispute resolution timeframe.
If the dispute is closed:\
  1. Review the notes in the dispute CRISS case and provide the customer with the outcome of the dispute.
  2. If you need additional details that aren’t available in the CRISS case, contact the Bank Operation Support Center (BOSC) to request information. Don't transfer the customer to the BOSC.

Update an existing dispute

\
\


Customers may contact PNC to add additional transactions or information to their dispute before the dispute is resolved.

When this happens, don’t cancel the original dispute. Follow the steps below.
 

\
  1. Don’t add comments in the dispute CRISS case after it’s been submitted because the department working the case won’t receive a notification that the case has been updated.
  2. If you need to:\
    • Add notes to the dispute case after it’s submitted, contact the Bank Operation Support Center (BOSC).\
      • If it’s outside of BOSC hours of operation, go to the next step.
      \
    • Add transactions to the dispute case after it’s submitted, go to the next step.
    \
  3. Submit a new dispute with the new or additional information/transactions. \
    • Don’t cancel the first dispute. 
    • If you’re adding transactions to a dispute case:\
      • If the first dispute was denied, include all disputed transactions in the new dispute.
      • If the first dispute is still open, include only the new transactions.
      \
    • In the new dispute comments, reference the first dispute CRISS case number. If the second dispute is for a:\
      • Debit/banking card, ACH or ATM transaction dispute: Include the CRISS case number of the first dispute.
      • Check or teller withdrawal transaction dispute: File a new Security Incident Report (SIR).
      \
    \
